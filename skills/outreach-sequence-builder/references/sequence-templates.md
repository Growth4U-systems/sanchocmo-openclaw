# Sequence Templates

> Reference data for outreach-sequence-builder skill. Proven cold outreach sequence structures with touchpoint-by-touchpoint breakdowns, timing, channels, and copy frameworks.

---

## Template Selection Guide

| Template | Best For | Touches | Duration | Channels |
|----------|----------|---------|----------|----------|
| 3-3-3 Classic | Enterprise, long sales cycle | 9 | 21 days | Email + LinkedIn + Follow-up |
| AIDA 4-Touch | Clear product, short cycle | 4 | 14 days | Email only |
| Value-First 5-Touch | Thought leadership brands | 5 | 12 days | Email + LinkedIn |
| Multi-Channel Blitz | High-value prospects | 7 | 14 days | Email + LinkedIn + Video |
| Signal-Triggered | Signal-rich environments | 5-7 (dynamic) | 14 days | Email + LinkedIn |

## Template 1: 3-3-3 Classic

**Best for**: Enterprise B2B, ACV > EUR 10K, long sales cycle (3-6 months)
**Philosophy**: Persistence with professionalism. Three waves, three channels.

### Wave 1: Email Introduction (Days 0-7)

**Touch 1 — Day 0: Cold Intro Email**
- Subject: [Signal-based or curiosity hook]
- Structure: 1 sentence context → 1 sentence pain → 1 sentence value → 1 sentence CTA
- Max length: 5 sentences
- CTA: Soft (interest check, not hard meeting ask)
- Example subject patterns:
  - "[Signal]: Quick thought on [pain point]"
  - "[Mutual connection] suggested I reach out"
  - "Question about [company]'s [specific challenge]"

**Touch 2 — Day 3: Follow-up Email**
- Subject: Re: [original subject]
- Structure: Reference Touch 1 → Add new value (insight, resource, data point) → Restate CTA
- Max length: 3-4 sentences
- Tone: Helpful, not pushy

**Touch 3 — Day 7: Value Drop Email**
- Subject: [Resource name] for [company]
- Structure: Share relevant content (article, benchmark, case study) → Connect to their situation → Soft CTA
- Include: Attachment or link to genuinely useful resource

### Wave 2: LinkedIn Engagement (Days 1-10)

**Touch 4 — Day 1: LinkedIn Connection Request**
- Note (300 char max): "[Context]. Saw [specific thing about them]. Would love to connect."
- Do NOT pitch in connection request

**Touch 5 — Day 5: LinkedIn Engagement**
- Action: Like + comment on their recent post (genuine, relevant comment)
- Or: Share their content with your network + tag them
- Purpose: Build familiarity before DM

**Touch 6 — Day 10: LinkedIn Direct Message**
- Structure: Reference previous interaction → Share specific insight for THEIR business → Ask for 15-min call
- Tone: Peer-to-peer, not salesperson

### Wave 3: Follow-up (Days 14-21)

**Touch 7 — Day 14: Case Study Email**
- Subject: "How [similar company] solved [same pain]"
- Structure: 3-sentence case study summary → Result with numbers → "Want to see if this applies to you?"

**Touch 8 — Day 18: LinkedIn Check-in**
- DM: "Hey [name], sent a couple emails about [topic]. Not sure if timing is right — happy to reconnect when it makes sense."

**Touch 9 — Day 21: Breakup Email**
- Subject: "Closing the loop"
- Structure: Acknowledge they're busy → Summarize value in 1 sentence → "If timing changes, here's how to reach me" → End positively
- Purpose: Leave door open, create urgency through withdrawal

## Template 2: AIDA 4-Touch (Email Only)

**Best for**: Clear product value prop, shorter sales cycles, SMB
**Philosophy**: Each email moves the prospect through one AIDA stage.

**Touch 1 — Day 0: ATTENTION**
- Subject: Pattern interrupt or curiosity gap
- Structure: Opening line that stops the scroll → Problem statement that resonates → Tease solution without revealing everything
- CTA: "Curious?"

**Touch 2 — Day 3: INTEREST**
- Subject: Re: [original] or "The [specific] approach"
- Structure: Expand on the problem → Share data/insight that demonstrates understanding → Hint at methodology/approach
- CTA: "Worth a 10-min look?"

**Touch 3 — Day 7: DESIRE**
- Subject: "[Similar company] results"
- Structure: Case study or social proof → Specific numbers and outcomes → "Imagine similar results for [their company]"
- CTA: "Let me show you how"

**Touch 4 — Day 14: ACTION**
- Subject: "Quick question, [name]"
- Structure: Direct and simple → "I've sent a few notes about [value]. Is this on your radar?" → Offer specific meeting times
- CTA: Calendar link or 2-3 time options

## Template 3: Value-First 5-Touch

**Best for**: Thought leadership brands, consultancies, agencies
**Philosophy**: Lead with genuine value. Never pitch until Touch 4.

**Touch 1 — Day 0: Gift Email**
- Subject: "[Resource] for [company]'s [challenge]"
- Structure: "I created/found this resource that I think is relevant to [their specific situation]" → Link/attachment → No ask
- Resource: Custom insight, relevant benchmark, tailored checklist

**Touch 2 — Day 3: Insight Email**
- Subject: "Noticed something about [company/industry]"
- Structure: Share specific observation about their business → What it means → What others in their position are doing
- Still no pitch

**Touch 3 — Day 5: LinkedIn Connection + Comment**
- Connect on LinkedIn with note referencing the emails
- Comment on their recent content with genuine insight

**Touch 4 — Day 8: Bridge Email**
- Subject: "An idea for [company]"
- Structure: Reference previous value shared → Bridge to how you can help specifically → Offer 15-min exploratory call
- This is the first ask

**Touch 5 — Day 12: Graceful Close**
- Subject: "Last thought"
- Structure: Quick recap of value shared → "If timing is off, no worries" → Leave door open → Suggest reconnect trigger

## Template 4: Multi-Channel Blitz

**Best for**: High-value prospects (top 50 accounts), ABM-style outreach
**Philosophy**: Maximum visibility across channels in 14 days.

**Touch 1 — Day 0: Email** — Personalized intro with signal-based hook
**Touch 2 — Day 1: LinkedIn** — Connection request with contextual note
**Touch 3 — Day 3: Email** — Value drop (content, insight, data)
**Touch 4 — Day 5: LinkedIn** — Engage with their content (like + comment)
**Touch 5 — Day 7: Email** — Case study or social proof
**Touch 6 — Day 10: LinkedIn DM** — Direct message with specific ask
**Touch 7 — Day 14: Video Email** — 60-second personalized Loom/video (say their name, reference their company specifically)

**Video email framework** (Touch 7):
- 0-10s: "Hey [name], quick video for you"
- 10-30s: "I noticed [specific thing about their company] and thought of [insight]"
- 30-50s: "[Similar company] did [result]. I think you could see something similar."
- 50-60s: "Would a quick chat make sense? Happy to share more details."

## Template 5: Signal-Triggered (Dynamic)

**Best for**: Teams using signal-definition + signal-monitor workflow
**Philosophy**: Right message at the right time based on buying signals.

### Base Sequence (if no signal detected)

Standard 5-touch Value-First template (Template 3 above)

### Signal Variants (swap into Touch 1-2 when signal detected)

**Signal: Fundraise**
- Touch 1 subject: "Congrats on the round — thoughts on scaling [area]"
- Angle: Post-funding = budget unlocked + pressure to scale fast
- CTA: Help them deploy capital effectively in marketing

**Signal: Hiring (VP Marketing / Head of Growth)**
- Touch 1 subject: "Saw you're hiring for [role] — quick thought"
- Angle: New hire needs 90-day win + proven frameworks
- CTA: Bridge gap while hiring, or augment new hire

**Signal: Tech Stack Change**
- Touch 1 subject: "[New tool] is a great choice — here's how to maximize it"
- Angle: Tool change = new initiative = budget + motivation
- CTA: Help them get ROI from new tool faster

**Signal: Competitor Mention**
- Touch 1 subject: "Alternative to [competitor] for [specific use case]"
- Angle: They're evaluating options, be part of the conversation
- CTA: Show differentiation specific to their use case

**Signal: Content Published**
- Touch 1 subject: "Loved your piece on [topic] — adding to the conversation"
- Angle: Engage with their ideas, establish peer relationship
- CTA: Continue the conversation over coffee/call

## Copy Frameworks for Individual Emails

### PAS (Problem-Agitate-Solve)
```
[Problem]: "Most [role]s at [company type] struggle with [pain]."
[Agitate]: "This leads to [consequence]. And the typical fix — [common solution] — often makes it worse."
[Solve]: "We help [similar companies] achieve [result] by [method]."
```

### BAB (Before-After-Bridge)
```
[Before]: "Right now, [company] is probably [current state]."
[After]: "Imagine instead: [desired state with specific metric]."
[Bridge]: "Here's how [similar company] got there: [method]. Want to explore if it fits?"
```

### QVC (Question-Value-CTA)
```
[Question]: "Quick question: [relevant question about their business]?"
[Value]: "I ask because [insight/observation]. Companies like [example] have found [result]."
[CTA]: "[Soft ask]"
```

## Timing Best Practices

| Parameter | Recommendation |
|-----------|---------------|
| Best send day (email) | Tuesday, Wednesday, Thursday |
| Best send time | 8-10 AM local time of prospect |
| LinkedIn activity | 7-9 AM or 12-1 PM |
| Minimum gap between touches | 2 days |
| Maximum gap between touches | 7 days |
| Total sequence duration | 12-21 days |
| Follow-up after no-reply | Wait 30 days, new angle |
