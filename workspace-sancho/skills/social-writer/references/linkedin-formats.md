# LinkedIn Formats Reference

## Platform DNA
- Sweet spot: 1,300-2,000 chars for posts, up to 3,000 for articles
- First line truncates at ~210 chars with "see more" → hook MUST be there
- Professional but human. Personal "I" stories with business lessons
- Links in first comment, NEVER in body (algorithm penalizes)
- No hashtags in body. Max 3 at very end if used at all.

## Formats That Work

### 1. Personal Narrative
"I [did/learned/failed at something]..."
- Open with vulnerable hook
- Build story arc (setup → tension → resolution)
- End with actionable lesson
- Best for: vulnerability, milestone, aha-moment signals

### 2. Listicle
"N things I learned about [topic] after [experience]"
- Numbers in first line (odd numbers perform better)
- Each point = 1-2 lines max
- Last point = strongest / most surprising
- Best for: system, metric signals

### 3. Contrarian Take
"Everyone says [X]. Here's why that's wrong."
- Bold opening that challenges conventional wisdom
- Provide evidence (data, experience, case study)
- Don't just disagree — offer the alternative
- Best for: contrarian, conflict signals

### 4. Case Study
"[Client/Company] went from [A] to [B] in [timeframe]. Here's how:"
- Challenge → Solution → Results → Key learnings
- Include specific numbers
- Make it replicable (actionable takeaways)
- Best for: milestone, metric signals

### 5. Framework/Playbook
"The [Name] Framework for [outcome]:"
- Visual structure (numbered steps, bullet points)
- Each step = actionable, not theoretical
- Include a "most people skip step N" callout
- Best for: system signals

## Posting Strategy
- 3-5x/week
- Best times: 08:30, 12:00, 17:30 (audience timezone)
- Engage in replies for 30 min after posting
- Carousel/document posts for higher reach (describe content if text-only)
