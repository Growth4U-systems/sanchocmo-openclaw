# SWOT Analysis — Concepts & Methodology

## SWOT Framework

| Quadrant | Source | Rule |
|----------|--------|------|
| **Strengths** | INTERNAL + CONFIRMED | Must be confirmed by Lens 3 (customer data) or multi-lens. NOT just company claims. |
| **Weaknesses** | INTERNAL + CONFIRMED | Must be backed by evidence (bad reviews, gaps, failed metrics). NOT hypothetical. |
| **Opportunities** | EXTERNAL | Market or competitive factors we can exploit. NOT internal capabilities. |
| **Threats** | EXTERNAL | Market or competitive factors that endanger us. NOT internal weaknesses. |

### Quality Bar

Each SWOT entry must have:
1. **Statement**: Clear, specific observation (not vague)
2. **Evidence**: Where this data comes from (which lens, which competitor, which trend)
3. **Impact level**: High / Medium / Low

**Bad**: "We have a good product" (vague, no evidence)
**Good**: "Customer review avg 4.6/5 across 200+ reviews, consistently praised for onboarding speed (self-intel Lens 3)" (specific, sourced)

### Common Mistakes
- Don't put "innovative" in Strengths unless customers confirm it
- Don't confuse internal weaknesses with external threats
- Don't list generic opportunities ("growing market") without specifics
- Don't duplicate across quadrants

## TOWS Matrix (Heinz Weihrich)

| Strategy | Cross | Logic | Question |
|----------|-------|-------|----------|
| **SO** (Maxi-Maxi) | S × O | Leverage strengths to capture opportunities | "How can we USE [S] to EXPLOIT [O]?" |
| **ST** (Maxi-Mini) | S × T | Use strengths to defend against threats | "How can we USE [S] to NEUTRALIZE [T]?" |
| **WO** (Mini-Maxi) | W × O | Fix weaknesses to unlock opportunities | "How can we FIX [W] to CAPTURE [O]?" |
| **WT** (Mini-Mini) | W × T | Minimize weaknesses to avoid threats | "How can we REDUCE [W] to AVOID [T]?" |

### Strategy Quality Rules
- **Specific**: Not "improve marketing" but "Launch LinkedIn thought leadership targeting [ECP] pain point"
- **Actionable**: Convertible to task in 1-2 weeks
- **Connected**: References which S/W/O/T items
- **Measurable**: Has verifiable expected outcome

**Minimum**: 2 per quadrant (8 total). **Ideal**: 3-4 per quadrant (12-16).

## ICE Prioritization

| Dimension | Measures | Scale |
|-----------|----------|-------|
| **Impact** | Needle movement | 1-10 |
| **Confidence** | Certainty | 1-10 |
| **Ease** | Implementation speed | 1-10 |
| **ICE Score** | (I + C + E) / 3 | 1-10 |

## Evidence Sources

### From Self-Intelligence (S + W):
- **S**: confirmed_strengths, top_pros (Lens 3), unique assets
- **W**: confirmed_weaknesses, top_cons (Lens 3), perception_reality_gaps, priority_fixes

### From Competitor Intelligence (O):
- universal_claim_reality_gaps, aggregate_unmet_needs, unused_positioning_angles, unexploited_channels, per-competitor vulnerabilities

### From Market Intelligence (O + T):
- **O**: trends (opportunity), adjacent_markets, regulatory opportunities
- **T**: trends (threat), dominant competitors, regulatory constraints, market maturity, economic factors

---

## Lite vs Deep Criteria

**Lite**: 4-quadrant SWOT with evidence + 8 TOWS strategies + top 3 identified
**Deep**: All Lite + 5+ entries/quadrant with impact + 12-16 TOWS ICE-scored + user validated + cross-references

---

## Edge Cases

- **Low confidence upstream**: Proceed but flag. "SWOT basado en datos de baja confianza."
- **No clear strengths (pre-launch)**: Look for POTENTIAL strengths (team, approach, niche focus)
- **SWOT matches competitor**: Dig deeper into Lens 3 customer perception
- **Too many items**: Top 5-7 per quadrant by impact
- **User disagrees**: User wins for S/W (internal). Check evidence for O/T (external).
