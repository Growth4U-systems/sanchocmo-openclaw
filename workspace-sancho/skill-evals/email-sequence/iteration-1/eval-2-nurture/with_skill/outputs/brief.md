# Lead Nurture Sequence: State of DevOps 2024 Report

## Sequence Overview

```
Sequence Name: DevOps Report Lead Nurture
Trigger: User downloads the "State of DevOps 2024" report (gated PDF)
Goal: Book a demo of the CI/CD platform
Length: 8 emails
Timing: Over 21 days (immediate → day 21)
Exit Conditions:
  - Books a demo (primary exit → moves to sales sequence)
  - Unsubscribes
  - Starts a free trial (moves to onboarding sequence)
  - Becomes inactive after email 8 (moves to re-engagement after 30 days)
```

## Audience Context

- **Who**: DevOps engineers, engineering managers, platform engineers, VP/Directors of Engineering
- **Trigger**: Downloaded the "State of DevOps 2024" report — indicates active interest in DevOps best practices and industry trends
- **What they know**: Aware of CI/CD concepts, likely evaluating or optimizing their current toolchain. Varying awareness of our platform specifically.
- **Relationship**: First meaningful interaction — they've exchanged contact info for content but haven't engaged with product yet.

## Strategy

This sequence follows the **Value Before Ask** principle. The report download signals problem-awareness ("I want to improve my DevOps practices"). We'll nurture from problem-aware → solution-aware → product-aware → demo-ready.

### Progression Logic
1. **Emails 1-2**: Deliver value, establish credibility (days 0-3)
2. **Emails 3-4**: Deepen the problem, introduce framework (days 5-8)
3. **Emails 5-6**: Social proof, show solution (days 10-14)
4. **Emails 7-8**: Handle objections, direct offer (days 17-21)

### Timing Rationale
- B2B audience → weekday sends only (Tue-Thu preferred)
- Early emails closer together (1-3 day gaps) to build momentum while report is top-of-mind
- Later emails spaced out (3-4 day gaps) to avoid fatigue as we shift to conversion

### A/B Testing Plan
- **Phase 1** (first 2 weeks): Test subject line variants (A/B/C) on emails 1-3
- **Phase 2** (weeks 3-4): Test send times (9am vs 11am vs 2pm)
- **Phase 3** (month 2+): Test email length and CTA placement on highest-traffic emails
