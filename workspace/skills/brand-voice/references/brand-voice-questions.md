# Brand Voice — Analysis Checklist & Strategic Questions

Reference document for brand-voice skill Full mode. Two sections: (1) Extract mode analysis checklist, (2) Build mode strategic questions.

---

## Extract Mode: 6 Pattern Categories

When analyzing existing content (3-5 pieces), systematically check these categories:

### 1. Tone Patterns
- Formal ↔ Casual (contractions? slang? sentence fragments?)
- Serious ↔ Playful (humor? lightness? gravity?)
- Reserved ↔ Bold (hedging? strong claims? confidence level?)
- Distant ↔ Intimate (we/they vs I/you? personal stories?)

### 2. Vocabulary Patterns
- Industry jargon level (heavy, light, translated for non-experts?)
- Signature words or phrases they repeat across content
- Words they consistently avoid
- Edgy language, profanity, or strong expressions?
- Formal words vs everyday equivalents

### 3. Rhythm Patterns
- Average sentence length (short punchy vs flowing)
- Paragraph length (1-2 sentences or 3-5?)
- Mix of short + long (or consistently one style?)
- Use of fragments as style
- List and bullet usage frequency

### 4. Structural Patterns
- How they open (story? question? bold statement? data?)
- How they transition between ideas
- How they close (CTA style? summary? open loop? question?)
- Headers and formatting preferences
- Visual hierarchy patterns

### 5. Personality Signals
- Self-deprecating or confident?
- Teacher or peer or rebel?
- Polished or raw?
- Optimistic or realistic?
- Types of references and examples used (pop culture, data, analogies, personal stories)

### 6. POV Patterns
- First person: I (personal) or We (company)?
- How they address reader: you? folks? friends? team? professionals?
- Direct address or general statements?
- Relationship stance: peer, mentor, authority, insider, outsider?

## Build Mode: 15 Strategic Questions

### Block 1: Identity (who the brand IS)

1. **Core personality**: What are 3-5 words that describe your brand's personality? (Not what you sell — how you show up.)
2. **Core belief**: What do you stand for? What's your fundamental belief about your industry?
3. **Origin**: What's the founding story or background that shaped how you see things?
4. **Differentiation**: What makes you genuinely different from others in your space — not in product, but in worldview?

### Block 2: Audience (who they talk to)

5. **Primary audience**: Who are you talking to? Be specific about role, context, and mindset.
6. **Tone resonance**: What communication style resonates with them? What makes them pay attention?
7. **Trust and turn-offs**: What would make them trust you instantly? What would make them click away?

### Block 3: Positioning (how they position)

8. **Archetype**: Are you the expert, the peer, the rebel, the guide, the insider, the friend, the provocateur?
9. **Accessibility spectrum**: Where do you sit between accessible ↔ exclusive?
10. **Authority spectrum**: Where do you sit between approachable ↔ authoritative?

### Block 4: Aspiration (voice models)

11. **Admired voices**: Name 2-3 brands or people whose communication you admire. What SPECIFICALLY do you like about how they communicate?
12. **Anti-models**: What do you explicitly NOT want to sound like? (Specific brands or styles to avoid)

### Block 5: Practical (specific preferences)

13. **Signature language**: Any words or phrases that are already signature to your brand?
14. **Banned language**: Any words or phrases you hate or want to explicitly avoid?
15. **Style boundaries**: How do you feel about humor? Profanity? Hot takes? Emojis?

---

## Build Pipeline: Questions → Voice Profile

After collecting answers, follow this synthesis sequence:

### Step 1: Synthesize Personality
From Identity + Audience answers → extract 3-4 core personality traits. Each trait should be specific enough to guide writing decisions.

**Bad trait**: "Professional" (too vague)
**Good trait**: "Calm confidence — speaks from experience without needing to prove anything. Never hedges, never brags."

### Step 2: Define Tone Spectrum
From Positioning + Aspiration answers → position on 5 dimensions:

| Dimension | Scale | Source |
|-----------|-------|--------|
| Formal ↔ Casual | 1-5 | Q2 + Q9 + Q15 |
| Serious ↔ Playful | 1-5 | Q6 + Q15 |
| Reserved ↔ Bold | 1-5 | Q10 + Q12 |
| Simple ↔ Sophisticated | 1-5 | Q5 + Q6 |
| Warm ↔ Direct | 1-5 | Q7 + Q8 |

Each position must include a qualifying note: "Casual, but not sloppy" is far more useful than just "Casual."

### Step 3: Set Vocabulary Rules
From Practical answers + trait synthesis:
- **USE list**: 10-15 words/phrases aligned with personality (include signature phrases from Q13)
- **AVOID list**: 10-15 words/phrases that feel off-brand (include banned words from Q14, anti-model patterns from Q12)
- **Jargon level**: Derived from audience's technical sophistication (Q5 + Q6)
- **Style boundaries**: Humor/profanity/emoji rules from Q15

### Step 4: Establish Rhythm
From Aspiration models (Q11) + personality traits:
- Sentence patterns (short punchy, flowing, mixed)
- Paragraph length norms
- Opening patterns (how to start content)
- Formatting preferences (headers, lists, bold, emoji)

### Step 5: Create "Do This / Not That" Pairs
The most critical output for AI consumption. Write 5-10 paired examples:
- Each pair shows the SAME idea expressed on-brand vs off-brand
- Include WHY the off-brand version fails (which trait it violates)
- Use real-world contexts (social post, email opener, headline — not generic sentences)
- These pairs become the core of the AI Brand Kit

**Good pair**: ✅ "Your growth is stuck. Let's fix that." → ❌ "Struggling with growth? We have the solution!" (violates: calm confidence — too salesy, too generic)

### Step 5b: Create Example Library
Write 3+ on-brand and 3+ off-brand standalone examples:
- On-brand examples should embody ALL traits simultaneously
- Off-brand examples should each violate ONE specific trait (and explain which)
- Use real-world contexts (not generic sentences)

### Step 6: Define Boundaries
From anti-models (Q12) + turn-offs (Q7) + banned language (Q14):
- Explicit DO's and DON'Ts (5+ each)
- Each rule should be specific and actionable, not vague
- "Don't use corporate speak" is vague — "Don't say 'leverage', 'synergize', or 'holistic solution'" is actionable
